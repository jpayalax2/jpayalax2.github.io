Full UX/UI design of a recreational family event app

A complete creative and fully functional app design focused on the entertainment for families and communities.

Overview

Kidzkrew is a recreational application for mobile that unifies kid’s activities around Dublin and other cities. Kidzkrew allows people to stay in tune with current leisure activities, also offers alternative entertainment solutions and keeps families informed of a diverse range of events. Kidzkrew Aims to connect families through a dedicated social media environmentby being a centralized hub of information and allowing businesses to target a specific audience.

Problem

There's a lack of platform/service in the entertainment industry dedicated specifically to families events or activities. Social networks or different websites which holds kids events information do exist but they are not mobile user friendly neither hold enough information as a event hub family-friendly app would do, nor organize events for them. Another difficulty is the time implemented on finding events around social networks unless you are already informed of the best sites and groups of information, people need to spend time on searching and following certain companies or organizations in order to find this activities.

Figure 1.Kidzkrew’s app design.

This app was made in 8 months by following a brief which would include a timetable to ensure the goals would be reached at the end of this journey.

This project was based on the results of interviews and surveys made for groups of people located in Dublin city centre and Tallaght that are willing to assist to family-friendly events.

App structure

The app structure helped to understand how functionalities where going to be located around the app, would define how the userflow would be structured and dictate a direction of what wireframes should be focused on.

Figure 4. Kidzkrew’s low-fi app structure.

Wireframing & Prototyping

With the proper planning, I was able to confidently move into creating wireframes for the app. I took the decision to focus more on the functionality and structure of the app, opting for low-fidelity wireframes with very little detail. Ultimately, I could add design elements in later, what was important for the project, was that the product (above all) was clear and simple.

Figure 5. Kidzkrew’s low-fi wireframes.

User flow

These diagrams helped to discover new ways that tested users were using this prototype and that I would never considered. Also helped to have a wider understanding of how the app would work and catch broken features or missing components before the product was shown.


I created two different userflow versions; a gamified version which would show just the events to attend and the map with their locations and a gamified version where they would find more features like, the ability to create events and organize them, a panel of rewards, community chat, among others.

Figure 5. Kidzkrew’s userflow.
User testing

User testing is a technique used in user-centered interaction design to evaluate a product by testing it on users. This can be seen as an irreplaceable usability practice, since it gives direct input on how real users use the system. So I decided to use this technique in the pornject with this tasks:


  1. Navigation controls;
  2. Events information/bookings;
  3. Swapping between Gamified and non-gamified version;
  4. Get rewards;
  5. Open inbox in community chat.

There's still some work to do on swapping between non-gamified and gamified version in app.

Design

The app design was conglomeration between colors, shapes, design styles and own ideas. The app design was based on a good structure to showcase events to users. By grouping them into categories and adding different features within the app, I created a simple and clear hierarchy users could learn and follow.

It plays with transparencies and negative spaces combining different colors and characters. Flat design is essential to reach a minimum use of style elements giving the illusion of three dimensions making the design simpler and spacious.

Yellow and green were chosen because they don’t identify any gender but is very neutral. The rest of the colors chosen are secondary colors needed for different illustrations, characters and shadows, shines and to identify in which section of the app is the user located.

To create different motifs, backgrounds and useful visual reinforcements I always used the last version of the logo. I was inspirated by my logo to create shapes, contours, negative spaces, and angles to create a diverse range of useful items that helps to expand the porject identity.
