Redesign of UX/UI Truwork App

A responsive enhanced UX/UI redesign of a task managment platform.

Overview

TRUwork is an end-to-end on-site risk managment application that allows peers to contact nearby or localized skilled proffesionals to solve your day-to-day problems.


It has its own managment services that categorizes different task processes in order to know relevance, amount of workers available, quality of each individual tasks, among others to secure and show outstanding solutions by demostrating to the customer and TRUteam the steps taken to fix the problem and its final results.

Problem

The main concern of this project was the display of information all aroud the application due to a lack of white space, user-focused experience, better layout and armonic design style.


The clutter on the previous version was a bit overwhelming for users and the design line wasn't the most adecuate which was creating an uncomfortable usage. Besides this, there was a lot of information and repetition of features which rendered a wrong layout which would make it even heavier and not aquarying the right feel and look that the project deserved.

Figure 1.TRUwork’s dashboard design.

The progress of this project was followed by the brief, strategy and changes that were being implemented by the client. Each stage of every change would take an approximate of a month in order to design it, develop it, test it and getting some feedbacks from real customers.

Research & Analysis

An extensive research market was the base to understand what would be the usability, feel and look of different dashboards and how would be extended through the responsiveness of all devices. Determining the right information by studying current companies on the live market like Jira, which was a helpful example, would be essential to make this project a success.


When having enough knowledge collected about UX/UI on dashboards-focus, the next step was to organize, categorize, differenciate and study the relevance of each of the personas that were potentially on use to enhance the usage of their features, actions, userflows while getting rid of unrelevant information and leaving the KPIs and core functionalities on its place.


The third and last step was the comparison of the previous version and the material found on the research in order to change the visuals, layouts and push out a new and improved design line for a better understanding. On this stage, simplicity would take the lead by dimminuting uncomfortable usage and irrelevant features. The project would lean more on testing results to boost every change made and make the most of it.

User scenarios & Personas

This project had already a brief that contained all the personas and when I started the project the PM had already a version of the user scenarios which helped me to upgrade it to its next version. The evolution of the platform revealed that there was much more to work in each individual screen rather than on the entire picture of the project.

UX Workshops and whiteboarding

To achieve most of the results I gathered a team with different PMs, developers and myself to find and collaborate in order to get knowledge from different industries, personalities and combine them while having the right solutions. The goals of these workshops were not just to achieve a better clarity and understanding of the layout, personas and functionalities but also to agree with the best solutions we could considerate as ideal. The results were an enhance of the user experience and feel and look but also we made sure that the core functionalities would remain intact.


On my research, I found out that there was a confussion between all the parts as to the Personas. When accesing to the site as different people with different permission some of them would have permissions that they shouldnt've and so on which made me understand each of the users we would have and created a discussion about it which ended up on a categorized user table that differentiated them to improve their usability.


Every decision was captured in a whiteboard.

Figure 2. Truwork’s whiteboarding.

Wireframing & Prototyping

With the proper planning, we were able to confidently move into creating wireframes for the app. We took the decision to focus more on the functionality and structure of the app, opting for low-fidelity wireframes with very little detail. Ultimately, we could add design elements in later, what was important for our users, was that the product (above all) was clear and simple.

Figure 3. TRUwork’s sketches.

User flow

There was a lot to do for this project both desktop and mobile versions so we decided to work along and have few workshops to understand mostly the userflows from the mobile versions due to needed a bit of a tweak. This ended up in wide spreads of mobile screens and discussions to find the correct flow.

Figure 4. TRUwork’s low-fi wireframes.
User testing / QA testing and A/B testing

Every decision taken was tested by real users. The results of the testig process would constantly create more work to enhance the product usability. Beside the real testing, we had a team that would be verificating every decision and upgrade of the platform and also we would carry different a/b tests to implement the best results.

Design

There was an entire creation of a Design system that would help for the future usage of the design and simplify the understanding of the platform. This design system will keep an order and consistancy among all the design elements and components.


The previous version of the platform needed a renewal by making it lighter to the eye and creating a stro0nger connection ammong its consistancy and design line. The creation of iconography and visual elements like its motifs and patterns helped to make it more comfortable and likeable.


From Dark to light grey the platform started getting more life and felt visually much more attractive. The implementation of the new design elements and colors blended as well as for the rest of the brand identity which optimizated the design and was definitely a 100% improvement.

Figure 5. TRUwork’s previous designs.

Figure 6. TRUwork’s previous designs.

Results

TRUwork achieved all its goals by creating a totally functional platform and all the improvements were implememented leaving its users really excited. The next step for the TRU would be an entire change of its strategy which would stop the design and development for a while in order to reach more industries and customers.


We achieved to deliver not just a desktop version of the platform but all its responsive iterations among every device.


We managed to reach all the expectatives of the client creating a full functional platform.

The company's founders are planning to further improve their service, so they will still have a lot of work to do on their platform.
